Stewed vegetables in a pan

Total cooking time: 30 min

Ingredients:

  • 1 piece Zucchini (All medium-sized vegetables)
  • 1 pc Bell pepper
  • 1 pc Carrot
  • 1 pc Onion
  • 12 pcs Cherry tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon Sunflower oil
  • Italian herbs — to taste
  • Salt — to taste

Step 1: Wash the zucchini, cut it lengthwise, then into large half rings — they should stay visible in the dish. You can substitute with regular zucchini. Place the sliced vegetables in a bowl.

Step 2: Peel the bell pepper and peel off the seeds and stalks. Cut the pepper into rather large pieces. It would be better to choose a red or yellow pepper for the external harmony of the finished dish.

Step 3: Wash the carrots by brushing the peel if it is heavily soiled. Peel the carrots with a vegetable peeler and cut into thin half rings.

Step 4: Peel the onion and cut into thin half rings. It would be better to take white or yellow onions. Purple will be discordant in the finished dish. You don't need to add onions at all if you don't like them.

Step 5: Wash the cherry tomatoes and cut them into thin rings. Actually, slicing for tomatoes can be absolutely any, only the appearance of the finished dish depends on it.

Step 6: Grease a non-stick frying pan with sunflower oil and put all the vegetables in it. Put it on medium heat.

Step 7: Salt the dish to taste, although this can be done at the end of cooking. Add your favorite spices or dried herbs. I have a mixture of Italian herbs.

Step 8: Simmer the dish under a closed lid over medium heat for five minutes, without stirring the vegetables.

Step 9: Stir the dish, remove the lid, and bring to a boil. Cook for 7 minutes for crisp veggies or up to 15 minutes for very soft ones. Enjoy!
