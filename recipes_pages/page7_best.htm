Chicken broth with noodles

Total cooking time: 1 hour

Ingredients:

  • 1 pc — Chicken (or any parts of chicken)
  • 2 pcs — Carrots
  • 1 pc — Onion
  • 0.5 cup — Vermicelli(noodles)
  • 2 L — Water
  • to taste — Salt
  • to taste — Ground black pepper
  • to taste — Fresh herbs

Step 1: Prepare: You can use a whole chicken or any parts like breasts, legs, thighs, or wings. I prefer using a soup chicken or rooster for a richer broth.

Step 2: Clean the chicken thoroughly, removing feathers if needed. Peel and rinse the onion and carrots. Place everything in a pot, cover with water, and bring to a boil. Skim off the foam, reduce heat, and simmer until the chicken is cooked through.

Step 3: Remove the solids: Take the chicken, onion, and carrot out of the broth. You can now use the clear broth as is, or add more vegetables for extra color and flavor.

Step 4: Add vegetables: I like to add a small carrot, thinly sliced into rounds or sticks. You can also finely chop a quarter of an onion for extra taste.

Step 5: Simmer the vegetables: Add them to the boiling broth and cook over medium heat for about 5 minutes.

Step 6: Add vermicelli and salt: Then let the soup finish cooking. If using fine vermicelli, I turn off the heat right after adding it, cover the pot, and let it sit on the hot stove for 5–10 minutes until fully cooked.

Step 7: Add chopped fresh herbs and ground black pepper to taste.

Step 8: Let the chicken cool slightly, then remove the meat from the bones.

Step 9: Place a few pieces of chicken into each bowl and pour over the hot broth.

Step 10: Serve the soup with boiled egg halves for a heartier meal.
