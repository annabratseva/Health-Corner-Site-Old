Philadelphia rolls with salmon

Total cooking time: 30 min

Ingredients:

    Rolls:

  • 10 g — Nori (2 sheets)
  • 200 g — Lightly salted salmon
  • 200 g — Cream cheese (preferably Philadelphia or any soft cheese)
  • 150 g — Cucumber or avocado (optional)
  • 1 cup — Short-grain rice

    • For cooking the rice:

  • 250 ml — Water

    • Rise seasoning:

  • 3 tsp — Rice vinegar
  • 0.5 tbsp — Sugar
  • 0.5 tsp — Salt

    • For serving:

  • 5 g — Sesame seeds (optional)
  • 10 ml — Soy sauce

Step 1: Prepare all ingredients. Use short-grain rice for sushi, fresh cucumbers, and lightly salted or raw salmon.

Step 2: Rinse the rice thoroughly, changing the water 5 times until it runs clear.

Step 3: Combine rice with water (1:1.25 ratio), bring to a boil.

Step 4: Reduce heat to low and cook covered for 15 minutes. Do not open or stir.

Step 5: While cooking, mix rice vinegar with salt and sugar to make the rice seasoning.

Step 6: The seasoning is ready.

Step 7: Slice the cucumber into thin strips. Leave the skin if it's tender.

Step 8: After cooking, let the rice sit covered for another 10 minutes.

Step 9: Add the seasoning and gently mix with a wooden spatula. Let the rice cool.

Step 10: Your sushi rice is ready.

Step 11: Cut a nori sheet in half. Wrap the bamboo mat with plastic wrap.

Step 12: Wet your hands and spread a thin layer of rice on the rough side of the nori.

Step 13: Flip the nori so the rice is facing down.

Step 14: Spread cream cheese (preferably Philadelphia) along the center.

Step 15: Add cucumber strips (and avocado, if desired).

Step 16: Roll tightly using the mat.

Step 17: Thinly slice the salmon. For easier slicing, slightly freeze the fish first.

Step 18: Place the salmon slices on the mat, then roll again so the salmon covers the roll. Cut into 6–8 pieces with a wet knife.

Step 19: Serve with soy sauce and sprinkle with sesame seeds if desired. Enjoy!
